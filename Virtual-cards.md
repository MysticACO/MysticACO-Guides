# Virtual Cards (VCC's)

## **Privacy.com**

* Sign-up cost: Free personal plan; paid tiers available for higher usage.
* Merchant-lock: Yes, built-in — can create single-use or merchant-locked cards.
* Free plan: up to \~12 new cards per month
* Paid plans: increase to 60–120+ new cards per month (varies by plan).
* Limits: Customizable per card (spending caps, pause/delete options).
* Extras: Great browser and mobile app integration; cards can be paused, closed, or renamed instantly.

{% hint style="info" %}
Best for: Personal users and light resellers. Not recommended for retail sites.
{% endhint %}

## **Capital One Eno (Virtual Card Numbers)**

For resellers, this is appealing because you can generate a unique card for each merchant, helping isolate transactions and track spend. This is a strong option for resellers who want merchant-specific cards and control.

* Sign-up cost: Free (available to eligible Capital One credit card holders).
* Merchant-lock: Yes — each virtual card number is automatically locked to the first merchant it’s used at.
* Number of cards: No published limit, but practical use shows hundreds of merchant-specific cards can be active.
* Limits: Each VCC shares the main card’s limit; individual cards can be locked or deleted anytime.

{% hint style="info" %}
Best for: New resellers that are looking to scale. Works on the majority of retail sites.
{% endhint %}

## **American Express (Amex)**

The strength here is the prestige/issuer backing and potentially higher underlying credit limits, especially if you have a business card.

* Sign-up cost: Free (requires an active Amex card account).
* Availability: Offered mainly through Amex Business and Corporate programs (some consumer cards may have limited access).
* Merchant-lock: Not specifically “merchant-locked,” but business accounts can set spend controls, limits, and expiration dates for each VCC.
* Number of cards: No publicly listed cap — depends on card type and account program (some business platforms allow hundreds).
* Limits: Controlled by your credit limit and internal Amex approval; per-card limits can be customized.

{% hint style="info" %}
Best for: Heavy resellers that want to scale their setup. No preset spending + Unlimited VCC's.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

## **Robinhood Gold VCCs**

Robinhood Gold offers a **credit card with built-in virtual card functionality**, allowing users to generate multiple card numbers from a single account. These virtual numbers can be used for online purchases and help separate transactions across different merchants.

* Sign-up cost: Requires an active Robinhood Gold subscription.
* Merchant-lock: Not strictly merchant-locked, but each virtual card number can be used independently.
* Number of cards: Robinhood allows multiple virtual cards to be generated. No limit.
* Limits: All virtual cards share the main credit card limit. Individual transaction limits may apply depending on account settings.

{% hint style="info" %}
Best for: Users already using Robinhood Gold who want simple virtual card generation without third-party VCC services.
{% endhint %}

## **Extend VCC's**

Extend allows users to create **virtual cards tied to an existing credit card account**. Instead of generating cards from a separate platform, Extend connects directly to your bank-issued card (such as Visa or Mastercard) and lets you generate additional virtual numbers for spending control.

• Sign-up cost: Free (requires an eligible credit card issuer that supports Extend).\
• Merchant-lock: No automatic merchant lock, but cards can have spending limits and expiration dates applied.

**Number of cards:**

• Depends on the issuing bank and Extend configuration\
• Many users can generate dozens of virtual cards per account

• Limits: Each virtual card draws from the primary credit card’s limit. Spending caps can be applied per card.

{% hint style="info" %}
Best for: Users who already have a strong credit card setup and want to generate additional virtual cards without opening new credit lines.
{% endhint %}
